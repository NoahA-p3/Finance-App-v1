# Delivery Phases

Related docs: [Product Module Map](./PRODUCT_MODULE_MAP.md), [MVP Scope](./MVP_SCOPE.md), [PRD](./PRD.md), [Epics](../../tasks/EPICS.md).

This phase plan is a product-priority view. It is not a claim that all listed items are implemented.

## Phase 1 — Foundational credibility
Goal: deliver a coherent bookkeeping baseline for day-to-day use.

### Priority capabilities
1. User and company setup fundamentals
2. Contacts and products catalog basics
3. Invoice, quote, and credit note baseline
4. Receipt upload and expense posting
5. Chart/category structure with VAT-ready tagging
6. Dashboard and report essentials
7. Basic role-aware access patterns
8. Visible support entry points

### Success outcome
A small business can complete core bookkeeping loops with clear records and review workflows, even with limited automation.

## Phase 2 — Platform parity expectations
Goal: move from baseline bookkeeping to integrated accounting workflows.

### Priority capabilities
1. Bank import/sync and reconciliation workspace
2. Recurring invoices and reminder sequences
3. Invoice payment method orchestration
4. Assistant-driven task and suggestion queues
5. Integration marketplace and sync observability
6. Migration intake and onboarding completion guidance

### Success outcome
The product feels operationally complete for ongoing monthly workflows, not only initial setup.

## Phase 3 — Advanced workflows and retention
Goal: add high-retention workflows that reduce end-of-period friction.

### Priority capabilities
1. Payroll module and payroll-linked postings
2. VAT submission and filing support workflow
3. Year-end workspace and filing-assistance features
4. Personal-business tax flow support
5. Developer platform and extensibility tools
6. Partner/advisor collaboration and service handoffs
7. Higher-confidence automation and learning suggestions

### Success outcome
The product supports advanced operational and compliance journeys through guided multi-step workflows.

## Shortest competitive feature baseline
If delivery scope must be reduced, keep these capabilities in the first serious release:
- invoices and quotes
- recurring invoices and credit notes
- contacts and products
- receipt inbox and expense posting
- bank reconciliation
- VAT review flow
- dashboard with task surfacing
- integrations and assistant tasks
- payroll connection path
- year-end workspace
